Head out to Killen Falls, it's around a half an hour drive and then just a 10 minute walk to the waterfall. Make sure you are in comfortable shoes as it can be a little slippery. The waterfall sits over a cave meaning you can walk behind it or set up in the shade whilst you're swimming beneath the waterfall.

The second stop is Minyon Falls. Minyon falls is a 45 minute drive North of Killen falls in Nightcap National Park. The waterfall itself is 100m and you can take an 8km circuit to see the top and bottom of the waterfall. However one thing to take in to consideration is the weather, If you take the 40 minute drive on a nice hot day in the middle of summer you may find one very dried out waterfall. Which could be a little frustrating, however at the top of the waterfall there were a few pockets of water that are deep enough to use as plunge pools overlooking the national park that don't should still be full.

You must stay away from the edge at the top of the waterfall as the rocks can be slippery and this could be extremely dangerous
