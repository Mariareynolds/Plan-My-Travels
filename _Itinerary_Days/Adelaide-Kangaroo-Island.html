Kangaroo Island is just off the peninsula from Cape Jervis which is roughly an hour and 40 minutes from Adelaide CBD. The transport from Adelaide CBD to Cape Jervis is limited so you will need to either drive or book the Sealink coach service. The most recent timetable can be found at Public coach timetable Tickets cost $29 for the coach one way and $49 for the Ferry from Cape Jervis to Kangaroo Island. Kangaroo Island Ferry If you are driving you can take your car on to the Ferry for $98 each way. The Ferry crossing takes roughly 45 minutes.

There are 2 ways to explore the island, you will either need to hire a car(or take your own car over on the ferry) or book a tour as there isn’t any public transport on the Island.

If you choose to hire a car, start your trip to Kangaroo Island by exploring the Islands wildlife. The Island is home to New Zealand Fur Seals at Cape de Couedic, Sea Lions in Seal Bay and Small Penguins at Pennshaw Penguin Centre. You should also keep an eye out for Koala’s and Kangaroo along the way.

If it’s a warm day you can head to a secluded swimming spot like Vivonne Bay or pick a quiet beach like Stokes Bay or Emu Bay to spend the afternoon.

If you’re looking for more active things to do on the Island you can book a Quad bike tour from $147 withKangaroo Island Quad Bike Tours or try some sand boarding and tobogganing from $37 at Little Sahara adventure centre

There are plenty of popular restaurants on the island, Pennshaw Beach and Kingscote both have a collection of Pizzeria’s, fish & chip shops and Australian restaurants.
