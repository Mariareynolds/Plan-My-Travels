The Museum of Sydney shows you how Sydney has changed over the years. The Sydney Museum is a modern museum built over and around the remains of Australia’s first Government House. The Museum of Sydney celebrates the people and events that have shaped the character and soul of this city. $15.00. Open Daily 10:00 – 17:00. The Museum is on the corner of Philip & Bridge Street.

Learn about how the convicts lived in the World Heritage listed Hyde Park Barracksh. A fascinating look into transportation and a nation built on a convict past. Modern Sydney is built on convict foundations – on convict labour, skills and stories. 50,000 convicts passed through its gates. Entrance is free however if you want an audio tour there is an additional cost. Opening times are 10:00 – 17:00 everyday.

Each year, Australians nation-wide commemorate Anzac Day on 25 April. The Anzac Memorial , located a few minute walk from our hotel in Hyde Park, is a place of commemoration and remembrance of the service and sacrifice of Australian men and women in war and peacekeeping activities since 1914.  The Memorial is open every day from 09.00 to 17:00. Entry to the Memorial is free of charge, though donations are welcome.

The Australian Museum is in Hyde Park. The museum shows you Australia over time, dinosaurs and their relatives , Earth Science and much more. The Australian Museum is open 09.30 to 17:00. Tickets cost $15.00 for the permanent exhibition and $23.00 if you also want to visit the popup exhibitions.

At the Maritime Museum you can climb aboard real-life tall ships, warships and a submarine and explore temporary exhibitions all centred on Australia and it’s relationship with the sea. The Museum is open 09:00 – 17:00 with last boarding time for vessels being 16:10. Located on Darling Harbour (The Prymont Side), the museum is a 25 minute walk from Town Hall. You can take A Ferry from Circular Quay or walk in 30 mins. A Big Ticket costs $25.00 and includes access to everything.
