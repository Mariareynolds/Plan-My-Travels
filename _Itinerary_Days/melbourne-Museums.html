A multicultural city with influences from all over world adding to the melting pot that is Melbourne.

The Immigration Museum is a museum primarily displaying Australia's immigration history. It is located on Flinders Street in Melbourne, Victoria, in the Old Customs House. Accessible on Free Tram or a short walk from Flinders Street or Southern Cross Stations. Entry is just $14.00 and the museum is open from 10:00am – 17:00pm.

Melbourne Museum showcases Australian social history, indigenous cultures, science and the environment. It is a nice walk from the City where you can check out the Carlton Gardens and Exhibition Building. It is also home to IMAX Theatre. Opening times are 10:00am – 17:00pm and entry is $15.00. IMAX tickets start from $15.00.

One of Australia’s most iconic figures was the bushranger Ned Kelly. Villain or hero, his story has lived on since his death at the Old Melbourne Gaol in 1880. The gaol runs various night tours including a ghost tour.

To see the original armour Ned Kelly wore in his last stand against the police you can visit the State Library of Victoria, opposite Melbourne Central Station. Visit between 10:00am and 17:00pm.
